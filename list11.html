List of US States

  1. New York
    • New York, specifically New York City, is a Mecca for culture, history, entertainment, art, music, and sports. Those looking for history can see the Statue of Liberty, Broadway for musical theatre, and Madison Square Garden for sports and concerts. The city is also full of some of the world’s best dining experiences. Of the top ten states that foreign travelers visited in 2013, New York accounted for 30.5% of the total visitors.
  2. Florida
    • Florida is the second-most visited stated by foreign travelers. There’s a lot to do in Florida, from beautiful beaches to amusement parks, including Disney World. The state also has consistent warm weather for most of the year and plenty of sunshine, warranting plenty of outdoor activities like beach trips, boating, and fishing.
  3. California
    • California is one of the most beautiful states in the country, with beautiful beaches and mountains for skiing, plus national parks for hiking. Additionally, tourists flock to Los Angeles to see Hollywood and with the hopes of spotting their favorite celebrities. Travelers also head slightly further north to see San Francisco and the Golden Gate bridge.
  4. Hawaii
    • Hawaii is the country’s piece of paradise. Who wouldn’t want to have a tropical getaway? Hawaii’s islands are full of pristine beaches, incredible volcanoes, and a beautiful culture that warmly welcomes visitors.
  5. Nevada
    • Nevada is the fifth-most visited state by foreign travelers in the United States. Aside from the Mojave Desert, Nevada is home to Las Vegas, perfect for those looking to gamble and immerse themselves in the wild nightlife. Las Vegas also has several one-of-a-kind shows, including music, comedy, and acrobatics. Las Vegas is also a hotspot for those looking to do some shopping.
